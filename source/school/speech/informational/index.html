History of Numerical Systems

Early Counting Systems

  • Counting with fingers and tally makes
  • Egyptians - 3000 BCE
    • | = 1
    • ^ = 10
    • |||^^ = 23
  • Roman Numerals
    • Adaption of the Egyptian system
    • I = 1
    • X = 10
    • XXIII = 23

Modern Counting Systems

  • Used by Babylonians around 1750 BCE
  • Base 60 system
  • No concept of zero

Decimal Systems

  • Egyptians used hyroglyph for "beautiful", nfr
  • Concept of zero as a number used by Indians around 300 BCE
  • Zero was refered to as sunya or "empty"/"void"
  • Arabic version was brought to Europe and quickly replaced Roman Numerals

Negative Numbers

  • Used in China and India from around 200 BCE
  • Weren't accepted in Europe until 17th Century
